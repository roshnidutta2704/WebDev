Why Do We Remember Some Dreams and Forget Others?

By Emilia Hartley

Published on October 9, 2022

Every night, we dream—sometimes vividly, sometimes not at all. But why do some dreams stay with us while others slip away the moment we wake up? This article explores the science of dream recall, including the roles played by REM sleep, the hippocampus, and the emotional content of dreams. It also dives into the psychological theories behind dream memory, such as Freud's ideas about repression and modern views linking dream vividness to neurotransmitter activity. With insights from neuroscience and cognitive psychology, this piece unpacks why some dreams leave a lasting imprint while others dissolve before we even open our eyes.

How Iceland Is Redefining Sustainable Tourism

By Dr. Leena Vyas

Published on February 18, 2024

Iceland has emerged as a global leader not just in natural beauty but also in responsible tourism. This article explores how the country has managed the tension between increased tourist traffic and environmental preservation. From limiting access to vulnerable areas like Fjaðrárgljúfur Canyon to implementing green energy solutions powered by geothermal and hydroelectric sources, Iceland is creating a model for sustainable travel. It also covers initiatives like the “Pledge for Nature” and how local communities are engaged in protecting Iceland's delicate ecosystems. Perfect for eco-conscious travelers and environmental advocates, this article showcases how tourism and sustainability can go hand-in-hand.

The Rise of Cottagecore: Escapism, Aesthetics, and Internet Subcultures

By Arjun Mehta

Published on June 3, 2023

Cottagecore is more than just pretty pictures of flower fields and vintage teacups—it's a full-blown cultural movement. Born on Tumblr and flourishing on TikTok and Instagram, cottagecore idealizes rural life, home crafts, and a return to nature. But beneath the surface of cozy aesthetics lies a deeper craving for simplicity and escape from fast-paced, digitally saturated lives. This article delves into the roots of cottagecore, its ties to gender expression and LGBTQ+ identity, and how it served as a comforting digital refuge during the pandemic. It also examines critiques of the trend, including its potential to romanticize privilege and overlook the realities of rural labor.
